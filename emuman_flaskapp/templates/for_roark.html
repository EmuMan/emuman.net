Hello Roark, as well as whoever else may be reading this page!

I am EmuMan, and you may remember me as the one who developed the trial editor and multiplayer mods.

This is an in-progress website that I made both as a way to explore web development and to showcase some of the stuff I have done over the years. Although it is not completely comprehensive, I hope that it will offer a general idea of some of the stuff that I do.

Among the more notable projects that are not listed on the website are, of course, the mods for RAoT that I have made. After watching Attack on Titan in early April, I discovered the game out of my own curiosity. Wanting to help with its development but realizing that applications had been closed, I decided to start writing mods for the game. Here are short showcases for some of the mods that I have made:

  • Custom Trials
  • Multiplayer (Unfinished)
  • Titan Shifting

After the new version of the game came out, I messed around with modding it. Since it was compiled using IL2CPP, this provided a whole new set of interesting challenges for me. I ended up writing a DLL that could be injected to allow for the speed cap to be removed and the camera to be shifted to first-person, but unfortunately I soon lost motivation after that and decided to move on to other things. I came back a little later to mod in the titan transformations, but I never really finished the multiplayer mod, although I have recently returned to it to rework the networking system I had in place. I also experimented with writing a website for the game, but I never really saw that through either, although I'd be happy to pick it up again if it is something that people think would be useful.

In regards to my experience with actual game development in Unity, I don't really have much. Most of it has been simulation work for my robotics team, and the majority of my knowledge of Unity (and C# in general) actually comes from my time modding RAoTFG.

There is one other project I am working on right now, which is a Minecraft server that you can find at the top of the Misc Programming page. My current work there revolves mostly around writing a new version of the website with fully-featured login, forum, and admin control systems, as well as better overall design. However, my efforts are not incredibly time-consuming, and I am confident that I can find the time to work consistently on both projects.

I encourage you to take a look at some of the stuff I have on this website if you are interested. It would be great to have a chance to work for the team.


Thank you for reading!
